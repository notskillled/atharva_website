Hackathon – The Mosquito Guard

First Place Winner

Description:

A low-cost, battery-powered ESP32 sensor system that monitors environmental conditions (temperature, humidity) made for communities heavily impacted by mosquito-borne diseases.

Problem

Communities in mosquito-prone areas lacked affordable, real-time monitoring tools to identify breeding hotspots. Manual surveys were slow, resource-intensive, and couldn't provide the timely data needed for effective intervention.

Impact

Mosquitoes are more than just a nuisance—they pose serious health risks by spreading diseases such as malaria, dengue, and Zika. Identifying areas with high mosquito activity is crucial to preventing bites, reducing disease transmission, and protecting communities. Our Mosquito Guard system detects and maps mosquito hotspots, enabling targeted interventions and smarter prevention strategies.

Features

  • $30 Build that targets real-time mosquito habitat detection
  • Accurately detects areas favorable for mosquito breeding
  • Python-based algorithms

Tech Stack

  • Python
  • ESP32 + Sensors: DHT22/BME280 for temp & humidity
  • Website + Data Analysis

Research & Planning

We researched mosquito breeding conditions and identified key environmental factors: temperature (25-30°C) and humidity (above 60%) create ideal breeding conditions. We mapped out sensor placement strategies and data collection intervals.

  • Studied mosquito lifecycle and breeding patterns
  • Identified critical environmental thresholds
  • Designed low-power sensor network architecture
  • Planned data visualization dashboard
Initial research and planning sketches
ESP32 sensor circuit design

Design & Prototyping

Created circuit designs for the ESP32 sensor nodes with battery optimization. Developed algorithms to analyze environmental data and predict breeding risk levels.

  • Designed weatherproof sensor enclosures
  • Prototyped power management system for extended battery life
  • Built initial sensor calibration routines
  • Created risk assessment algorithm
Initial Sensor Prototype
Simple enclosure CAD design

Build & Implementation

Assembled and programmed multiple sensor units. Implemented wireless data transmission and built a web dashboard for real-time monitoring and alerts.

  • Connected and assembled 5 sensor units
  • Programmed ESP32 firmware for data collection
  • Set up WiFi-based data transmission
  • Developed web interface for data visualization
Finished sensor unit with enclosure
Real-time monitoring dashboard

Challenges

We struggled a lot of time management, presenting our work to the Hackathon Judges, and struggled a lot to pull data from the sensors. I think from the future we should create a time schedule that allows us to make sure we are on track for projects that are tight on time.

Results & Learnings

Successfully created a working prototype for under $30 per unit. The system accurately identified high-risk areas 85% of the time. Learned valuable lessons about power management, sensor calibration, and the importance of user-friendly data presentation for non-technical stakeholders.

Wandr – DECA EIB Business Plan

Description:

For DECA's Entrepreneurship Innovation Business (EIB) competition, my partner and I developed a comprehensive 20-page business plan for Wandr, a smart travel planning app that creates full itineraries based on user preferences and adjusts plans in real-time. This project involved extensive market research, financial modeling, competitive analysis, and strategic planning to address one of the biggest issues in travel today: planning.

Executive Summary

"We're building the first adaptive travel platform - a smart planner that doesn't just plan your trip, but travels with you." Unlike static tools like TripAdvisor or Booking.com, our app dynamically updates itineraries in real-time, even offline. It personalizes every trip based on user preferences, allows group collaboration, and automatically replans around delays, weather, or cancellations.

The Problem We're Solving

  • 78% of travelers report planning-related anxiety
  • 65% abandon detailed planning due to time constraints
  • Trip plans feel messy across emails, screenshots, and multiple apps
  • Struggle to reorganize when timing changes (delays, weather, closures)
  • Group trips fail due to coordination issues across multiple platforms

Customer Segments & Market Research

Through extensive research, we identified three primary customer segments within the U.S. travel market where mobile and app-based planning is firmly established (85% of travelers use smartphones during trips).

  • Flexible Leisure Travelers (ages 20-40): Couples and solo travelers taking 2-6 trips/year, booking with OTAs, following travel social media, open to last-minute changes
  • Road-Trippers & Adventure Travelers: U.S. travelers, van life explorers, spontaneous planners needing offline functionality and dynamic rerouting
  • Family Planners: Parents managing complex itineraries with kids (Disney trips, national parks) who value backup plans and stress-free coordination (willingness to pay: HIGH at $10-15/month)
Three primary customer segments identified

Unique Value Proposition & Competitive Advantages

Wandr has two deeply differentiated competitive advantages that address core shortcomings of existing travel platforms:

1. Algorithm-Driven Dynamic Itinerary Planning:

  • Constructs cohesive, detailed itineraries accounting for budget, time, group size, and preferences
  • Real-time recalculation when conditions change (weather, delays, closures)
  • Persistent preference logic that learns from past trips and feedback
  • Eliminates manual reorganization across multiple tools

2. Unified Collaboration-Centered Platform:

  • Addresses fragmentation of group travel planning
  • Built-in chat, voting, cost-splitting, and shared itinerary editing
  • Eliminates need for messaging apps, shared docs, spreadsheets, and payment tools
  • Creates network effects - as more users collaborate, platform becomes more central
Wandr vs. competitors feature comparison

Revenue Model & Financial Projections

Developed a multi-stream revenue model with realistic projections based on market analysis and industry benchmarks.

Revenue Streams (Ranked by Importance):

  • 1. Investments: Early-stage funding for app development and growth (not recurring)
  • 2. Ads/Sponsorships: Hotels, restaurants, small businesses can be featured (builds credibility); long-term partnerships with travel companies, airlines, credit card brands
  • 3. Affiliate Commissions: 4-8% average commission on bookings (hotels via Expedia/Booking.com at 7%, activities, restaurants)
  • 4. Premium Subscriptions: $16/month or $145/year for Pro features

Pricing Strategy:

  • Basic (Free): Ads, 5-15% discounts at limited places (increases over time), no health app integration
  • Pro ($16/month or $145/year): No ads, 18-30% discounts at many more places, health app/Apple Watch/Samsung Watch integration for activity planning

Year 1 Financial Projections:

  • Target Users: 50,000 total users (13% converting to Pro = 6,500 Pro users)
  • Total Year 1 Revenue: ~$625,000 (Pro subscriptions, local business listings, booking commissions, ads, affiliate)
  • Total Year 1 Expenses: ~$868,000 (salaries, marketing, hosting, legal, distribution costs)
  • Net Profit Year 1: -$243,000 (break-even projected Month 18 with 12,000+ active users)
  • Monthly Break-Even: Achieved in November of Year 1 with positive cash flow

Detailed breakdown of how capital would be allocated across different business functions:

Monthly Operating Costs (Year 1 Average):

  • HR Costs: $41,000-47,000/month (Founders, Customer Service, Marketing Team, Maintenance Engineers, Business Development Managers)
  • Marketing (CAC Costs): $13,000-19,400/month (Paid ads, SEO, loyalty rewards, sponsorships)

  • LLC Formation: $1,000
  • Terms of Service/Privacy Policy: $1,000

  • Google Play Store Registration: $25
  • Total Startup Investment: $3,525

Go-to-Market Strategy & Customer Acquisition

Developed a comprehensive five-channel strategy to reach U.S. travelers efficiently:

  • App Store Optimization (ASO/PSO): iOS: 6.6% tap-through rate, $4-6 CPI with Apple Search Ads; Android: $3.28 CPI, 27-30% conversion rate via Google App Campaigns
  • Social Media Advertising: TikTok, Instagram, YouTube targeting ages 20-40; 71% of travelers influenced by social media when choosing destinations; 61% use social media for travel inspiration
  • Influencer Partnerships: Micro and mid-tier travel influencers (solo, couples, road-trippers); 49% of consumers rely on influencer recommendations for travel decisions
  • SEO & Content Marketing: 81% of travelers begin trip planning on Google; keyword-optimized blogs, destination guides, AI-planning tutorials for long-term organic growth
  • Referral & Loyalty Programs: Travel credits, free premium trials, discounts for referrals; 77% of app downloads driven by friend recommendations
Five-channel customer acquisition strategy + Customer acquisition cost analysis by channel

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Established four primary KPIs to measure business progress, product effectiveness, and platform success:

  • User Engagement: Active usage time, visiting frequency, core user actions (trips created, itinerary changes, backup plans selected)
  • Customer Satisfaction: In-app feedback, app store reviews, Net Promoter Score (NPS), support interactions
  • Collaboration Adoption: Percentage of collaborative trips, average collaborators per trip, frequency of shared itinerary updates (measures product differentiation)
  • Annual Revenue: Yearly income tracking subscriptions, premium features, affiliate commissions against projections

Use Cases & Real-World Scenarios

Developed detailed use cases demonstrating Wandr's value across different traveler types:

  • Last-Minute Traveler: Solo traveler lands in Rome with no plan, fills out preference survey, instantly gets full itinerary with restaurant reservations, cultural spots, and total trip cost
  • The Group Trip: Four college friends planning Hawaii trip - all use one app for chat, voting, shared payments, balanced preferences instead of texts, Google Docs, Venmo, and multiple sites
  • Smart Return Traveler: User's past Japan trip ratings (loved cultural tours, disliked nightlife) automatically inform Thailand trip planning without re-survey
  • International Planner: Couple planning Europe trip - app checks visa requirements, provides application links, stores passport/insurance details securely

Puzzle Box – High-Speed 3D Printer

Description: A custom-built high-acceleration 3D printer controlled by a Raspberry Pi cluster, designed to achieve faster print speeds while maintaining quality through distributed processing.

Features

  • Raspberry Pi cluster for distributed print processing
  • High-acceleration motion system (up to 10,000 mm/s²)
  • Custom firmware for parallel computation
  • Advanced cooling system for high-speed printing

Tech Stack

  • Raspberry Pi 4 cluster (4 nodes)
  • Python for cluster coordination
  • Modified Klipper firmware
  • Custom linear rails and motion components

Problem

Standard 3D printers are limited by single-board processing power, restricting print speeds and complex calculations. High-acceleration movements require real-time processing that most controllers can't handle, leading to quality issues at high speeds.

Research & Planning

Researched distributed computing approaches and analyzed bottlenecks in 3D printing pipelines. Studied high-speed motion control systems and print quality factors.

  • Analyzed existing 3D printer architectures
  • Researched cluster computing for real-time applications
  • Studied motion control algorithms and acceleration limits
  • Planned workload distribution across Pi cluster
Motion system research and calculations
Raspberry Pi cluster architecture plan

Design & CAD Modeling

Designed custom frame and motion system in CAD. Created mounting solutions for the Pi cluster and optimized for rigidity to handle high accelerations.

  • Modeled entire printer frame in Fusion 360
  • Designed custom toolhead for weight reduction
  • Created Pi cluster mounting and cooling system
  • Simulated stress points and reinforced structure

Build & Assembly

Assembled the mechanical frame, wired the electronics, and set up the Raspberry Pi cluster with custom networking. Calibrated the motion system for precise high-speed movements.

  • Built aluminum extrusion frame with linear rails
  • Assembled and wired all electronics and motors
  • Configured Pi cluster with shared memory architecture
  • Installed modified Klipper firmware

Testing & Calibration

Performed extensive testing at various speeds and accelerations. Tuned motion parameters and validated print quality against standard printers.

  • Calibrated steps/mm and acceleration limits
  • Tested at incrementing speeds up to target acceleration
  • Compared print quality at high vs. standard speeds
  • Optimized cluster workload distribution

Results & Learnings

Achieved 3x faster print times compared to standard printers while maintaining comparable quality. The Pi cluster successfully handled real-time motion calculations. Learned about distributed systems, motion control, and the mechanical engineering challenges of high-speed 3D printing.
